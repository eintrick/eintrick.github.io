What are Bosons?

Bosons are in general the carriers of the 3 out of 4: Strong, weak and electromagnetic, fundamental forces. Photons are responsible for carrying the electromagnetic force to create the magnetic/electric fields and also let us see light, W+, W- and Z for the weak force, which makes the radioactive decay possible, and Gluon bosons help hold the atom nuclei together with the strong force. There are also, first found in 2012, Higgs bosons, that make up an omnipresent field, which gives all other particles when traveling through it mass. The only particles this rule is not applied to are the neutrinos, they still have mass, but it’s cause is currently unknown.
