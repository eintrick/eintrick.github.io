In physics, an elementary particle is defined as a particle without a currently known substructure. Fermions, which consist of quarks, leptons, antiquarks and antileptons, are all the matter particles, because all the matter we know as of now consists of them. For each of the quarks and leptons there are also their opposite - anti particles. Only less than 1% of the known universe consists of antimatter, but if antimatter meets regular matter the annihilation occurs, releasing lots of energy and likely radiation. The other group of elementary particles are bosons, those are responsible for all the physical forces and interactions between matter, except gravity. Gravity is theorized to have another, not yet found, particle or like in Einstein's theory - it's just the curve of the spacetime caused by mass and energy.
