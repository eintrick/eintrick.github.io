What are Leptons?

There are 2 main groups of leptons: The charged ones: Electrons, Tau and Muons, and chargeless: Electron, Tau and Muon neutrinos. Out of the charged leptons electrons have the lowest mass, hence are the most stable, while Tau and Muons can only be created by the large outburst of energy and then quickly decay into electrons and neutrinos. The leptons with a charge are affected by the electromagnetic, weak and gravitational force, but chargeless neutrinos interact with weak and gravitational ones. For a long time it was believed that neutrinos have no mass, because people couldn’t build a right device to catch them, but it turns out that they’re just incredibly light and the weak gravitational and weak forces weren’t strong enough to stop them from moving through. 1989 in Japan was built a first ever neutrino detector named: Super Kamiokande, which is a large underground room filled with water to catch the neutino’s interactions with matter.
